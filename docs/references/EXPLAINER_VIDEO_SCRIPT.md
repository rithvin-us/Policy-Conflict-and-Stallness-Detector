# Explainer Video Script — "From Tangled to Transformed"
### Sentinal — animated explainer (caterpillar → butterfly metaphor)

**Style:** flat 2D motion graphics, Kurzgesagt-style — simple geometric
characters, a warm/dark navy background with accent-color highlights (matches
the product's own dark console theme), smooth easing, light background music
that builds toward the end.
**Target length:** ~2 minutes. **Voice:** warm, plain-language narrator — no
jargon in the first half.

**The metaphor:** a company's policy corpus starts as a tangle of documents —
messy, contradictory, slowly decaying, like a caterpillar that's grown sluggish
and can't move efficiently under its own weight. Sentinal is the
chrysalis / transformation process. What emerges is lean, coordinated,
self-monitoring — a butterfly: still the same organization, but able to see
and correct itself continuously.

---

## Scene 1 — The tangle (0:00–0:20)

**[VISUAL]** A caterpillar made of dozens of small overlapping document-shaped
segments, each segment a different pastel color, crawling slowly and clumsily.
Some segments visibly point in opposite directions (little arrows), a couple
are grayed out / dusty (stale). It labors to move forward.

**"NARRATION":**
> "Every organization grows a body of policies over time — one for passwords,
> one for cloud, one for data retention — each written by a different team, in
> a different year. Slowly, this body gets heavier. Some rules quietly
> contradict each other. Some are just old. And the organization keeps
> crawling forward anyway, dragging all of it along."

---

## Scene 2 — The contradiction, zoomed in (0:20–0:40)

**[VISUAL]** Camera pushes into two adjacent segments of the caterpillar. One
segment glows and shows a tiny icon: a lock rotating with a "90" badge. The
segment next to it shows a lock with a shield and a "no rotation" strike-through
icon. The two icons visibly clash — a small spark/jitter animation where they
touch.

**"NARRATION":**
> "Here — one segment says 'rotate every ninety days.' Right next to it, another
> says 'don't rotate — MFA already covers it.' Neither team knew about the
> other's rule. This happens dozens of times across a real policy set, and it's
> invisible... until an auditor finds it first."

---

## Scene 3 — Enter Sentinal, the chrysalis (0:40–1:00)

**[VISUAL]** The caterpillar pauses, curls up, and a translucent chrysalis
shape forms around it — rendered as a soft glowing outline, almost like a scan
line sweeping top to bottom. Inside, small readable glyphs flow: "must,"
"shall," "should," little magnifying-glass icons passing over each segment.

**"NARRATION":**
> "Sentinal wraps around the whole corpus and reads it, sentence by
> sentence — every 'must,' every 'shall,' every 'should' — pulling out exactly
> what each rule requires, of whom, and under what conditions."

---

## Scene 4 — Inside the chrysalis: sorting (1:00–1:20)

**[VISUAL]** Inside the glowing outline, the document-segments rearrange
themselves into a clean grid, then colored threads draw between segments that
relate to each other — red threads for conflicts, yellow for redundancy, gray
dashed lines for "aging out." A small dial/gauge appears and fills partway,
like a health meter.

**"NARRATION":**
> "It compares every rule against every other rule, draws the connections a
> human would take weeks to find — direct contradictions, silent duplicates,
> rules that quietly went stale — and scores the whole organization's policy
> health, live."

---

## Scene 5 — Emergence: the butterfly (1:20–1:45)

**[VISUAL]** The chrysalis cracks open with a soft light bloom. A butterfly
emerges — its wings are made of the same document-segments, but now
symmetrical, color-coordinated, glowing gently, with a small clean dashboard
overlay visible on one wing (mini gauge, mini graph, mini checklist — echoing
the real product UI). It flies smoothly, none of the earlier jittering.

**"NARRATION":**
> "What comes out isn't a new set of policies — it's the same organization,
> now able to see itself clearly. Every conflict has an explanation and a fix.
> Every stale rule is flagged before it becomes a finding. And it keeps
> watching — every time a policy changes, it re-checks itself automatically."

---

## Scene 6 — Close / call to action (1:45–2:00)

**[VISUAL]** The butterfly settles on a small shield icon with the product
wordmark "Sentinal" beneath it. Tagline fades in underneath.

**"NARRATION":**
> "Sentinal. Turn a tangle of policies into something that governs
> itself."

## Production notes

- **Music:** start sparse/slightly dissonant (scene 1–2), swell gently through
  the chrysalis (scene 3–4), resolve to a warm, simple theme at emergence
  (scene 5–6).
- **Color language:** keep it consistent with the real product's dark theme —
  deep navy/ink background, one accent color (the product's `accent` teal/blue)
  for anything "good," warm red/amber only for conflict/staleness moments, so
  the metaphor visually rhymes with the actual dashboard judges will see next
  in the demo video.
- **Tools:** After Effects, or a simpler no-cost path — Canva/CapCut with
  built-in shape animations, or a Lottie-based animation if a designer is
  available. 2 minutes of clean 2D motion graphics is very achievable in 1–2
  days with a template-based tool.
- **Pacing:** this is deliberately paced slower than the demo video — it's the
  "why should I care" piece, meant to run *before* the live product demo, not
  instead of it.
